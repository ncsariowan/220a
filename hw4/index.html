Music 220A Homework #4: Effects and Patterns

by Nathan Sariowan

This chuck script models a sub-octave pedal commonly used with electric basses.

The script takes the logic of a comparator to find the frequency of the signal, and the logic of a d flip-flop to halve the frequency (and that frequency halved again for a second octave). The resulting square wave is then used to invert the original signal and thus create a natural-sounding suboctave effect. The signal is low-passed both before and after being octaved -- before to clean the signal and help the comparator find the frequency, and after to reduce the harmonics that result from the inversion. Logic is written at the sample level.

There are variables for the mix of the first octave, second octave and dry signal.
